Hello there!

My name is
Priscilla Nishiyama Kohlman and I'm an average girl.





I was born in São Paulo, Brazil to a Korean mom and a Japanese dad. When I was roughly seven years old, my parents decided to move to the states and I found myself in Tennessee. It wasn’t long before I picked up English and started adapting to my surroundings. Life was good.

That’s probably the exact reason why my family decided to move to Georgia. Priscilla is getting too comfortable here. Let’s kick up the difficulty level and make her cry. I'm partially convinced that’s exactly what they were thinking. It had nothing to do with the better schooling district and safer community.

Jokes aside, it was a little harder to move schools in the middle of 7th grade. So in addition to puberty, my teenage angst was at an all time high. Desperate for an outlet, I began blogging on a website called Xanga. I spent most of the time customizing and “coding” layouts instead of actually blogging. It wasn’t long before Myspace came around and I found myself another platform to tinker with. After a while, I began creating layouts for other people.

My most memorable iterations included designs without a “Top 8” section. I despised the concept of ranking my friends in a list and the unnecessary drama that came along with it. I figured it was a me problem so I kept that list hidden exclusively on my personal site. Still, it wasn’t long before I was bombarded with messages asking me how I did it and if I could pump out similar layouts. While I enjoyed creating content for others, the solution to remove that section was created to solve my problem and enhance my experience with the platform.

Fast forward to the future, my focus shifted to creating meaningful solutions for others. While working as a bartender, it was my job to ensure guests with a positive experience by fulfilling their needs and expectations. My ability to empathize helped me to better understand my customer base. I found satisfaction in providing quality service and receiving positive feedback. And though I found great value in my line of work, I longed for something a bit more fulfilling.

That’s where UX design comes into the picture. It encompasses my passion for empathizing with people, my creativity for problem-solving, and my ability to apply art processes to further enhance UX design. My experiences give me a unique approach to coming up with solutions for others. People are able to do amazing things with the help of technology when it is designed to work for them. I want to bridge the gap between humans and technology which, in turn, should yield amazing results.
